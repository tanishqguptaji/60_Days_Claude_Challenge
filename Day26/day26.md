# Day 26 – Prior Authorization Workflow Simulator

## Objective

Today's challenge focused on building an interactive **Prior Authorization Workflow Simulator** to understand how healthcare providers, patients, and insurance companies collaborate during the prior authorization process.

The goal was to transform a complex healthcare workflow into an engaging browser-based educational simulation using only HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

---

# Project

## Prior Authorization Workflow Simulator

A fully interactive web application that simulates the U.S. healthcare Prior Authorization (PA) process from patient intake to final payer decision.

The simulator uses drag-and-drop interactions, progress tracking, educational guidance, and multiple patient scenarios to make healthcare workflows easier to understand.

---

# Features Implemented

## Interactive Workflow

The simulator models three major participants:

* 👤 Patient
* 🏥 Provider / Clinician
* 🛡️ Payer / Insurance Company

Users progress through each workflow stage while completing the required authorization process.

---

## Patient Scenarios

Multiple healthcare scenarios were included, such as:

* Specialty Medication
* MRI Authorization
* Elective Surgery
* Inpatient Admission

Each scenario follows a different authorization journey.

---

## Workflow Stages

The simulator includes:

* Patient Intake
* Medical Necessity Evaluation
* Document Collection
* Prior Authorization Submission
* Insurance Review
* Approval / Pend / Denial
* Appeal
* Peer-to-Peer Review
* Final Decision
