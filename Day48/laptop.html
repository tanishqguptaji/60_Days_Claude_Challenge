How this was researched

Every figure below comes from manufacturer spec sheets, published lab reviews, or hands-on measurements from independent outlets. Where outlets disagreed, both figures are shown and the conflict is flagged rather than silently averaged.

Price

Sourced from manufacturer starting configurations and current review pricing (Tom's Guide, TechRadar, Tom's Hardware, and manufacturer sites). Prices reflect base/entry configurations as reviewed, not maxed-out builds — actual street price fluctuates with sales.

Battery life — where sources conflict

  • MacBook Air M3: Apple claims 15–18 hours; Laptop Mag's lab test measured "over 15 hours" of real-world uptime — sources agree closely.
  • Dell XPS 13 9345: Dell markets "up to 27 hours," but independent lab tests diverge sharply — Reviewed.com measured 18h38m and LiveScience measured 27h39m on different test methodologies. We report the independently tested range rather than the marketing figure alone. CONFLICT NOTED
  • ThinkPad X1 Carbon Gen 12: XDA Developers found battery life "fantastic," matching the prior generation, while Thurrott's real-world (non-benchmark) testing in daily use averaged only ~5h12m — a large gap likely driven by workload/brightness differences. We show both. CONFLICT NOTED
  • ROG Zephyrus G14 (2024): SMBtech's PCMark 10 test measured 12h24m; MyFixGuide's stricter test conditions projected only ~5–6 hours. Gaming laptops are highly sensitive to test methodology — both figures are shown.

Performance

Where a directly comparable third-party benchmark (e.g., a single Geekbench/Cinebench suite run across all four machines) was not available, performance is scored qualitatively using each processor's class and reviewer consensus (Tom's Hardware, LaptopMedia, SMBtech, Reviewed.com) rather than invented benchmark numbers. These qualitative scores are flagged EST in the matrix.

Display, build quality, ports, weight

Pulled directly from manufacturer spec sheets (Apple, Dell, Lenovo, ASUS/ROG) and cross-checked against Notebookcheck and LaptopMedia spec databases.

What's an estimate

Only the composite "Performance" and "Build quality" scores are analyst judgments (marked EST) rather than a single sourced number, because no single benchmark run covers all four machines under identical conditions. Every other cell traces to a specific citation in the Sources panel below.
