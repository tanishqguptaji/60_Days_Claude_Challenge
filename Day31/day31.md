# Day 31 – AI Supply Chain Control Tower

## ABTalks 60 Days Claude Challenge

## Project Overview

Today I built **AI Supply Chain Control Tower**, an interactive enterprise operations simulator that recreates the experience of managing a live global supply chain.

The application places the user in the role of **Head of Operations**, where multiple real-time supply chain disruptions occur simultaneously. Every operational decision directly affects key business KPIs including service level, customer satisfaction, inventory health, transportation efficiency, operating cost, and revenue protection.

Designed as a premium dark-themed enterprise dashboard, the simulator combines operations management concepts with interactive gameplay to help users develop faster and more strategic decision-making skills.

---

# Objective

Build a complete offline HTML application that simulates a real-world Supply Chain Control Tower where users monitor disruptions, prioritize incidents, and protect business performance under time pressure.

---

# Features Implemented

## Executive Introduction Screen

The simulator begins with a mission briefing that introduces the player as the newly appointed **Head of Operations**.

The introduction explains:

- Mission objective
- Game duration
- Alert escalation
- Scoring methodology
- Decision-making rules

This onboarding ensures even beginners understand how enterprise control towers operate before entering the live dashboard.

# Live Enterprise Dashboard

The main control center displays continuously updating operational KPIs.

### Live Business Metrics

- Service Level
- Customer Satisfaction
- Inventory Health
- Transportation Efficiency
- Operating Cost
- Revenue Protected
- Overall Score

# Dynamic Alert Feed

The simulator continuously generates operational disruptions that require immediate action.

Examples include:

- 🚨 Port Congestion
- 🚨 Supplier Delay
- 🚨 Truck Breakdown
- 🚨 Warehouse Running Out of Stock
- 🚨 Customs Inspection
- 🚨 Demand Spike
- 🚨 Factory Machine Failure
- 🚨 Weather Disruption
- 🚨 Wrong Inventory Count
- 🚨 Damaged Shipment

Each alert contains:

- Title
- Description
- Priority Level
- Countdown Timer
- Business Impact

Alerts become increasingly frequent as the simulation progresses, forcing players to prioritize effectively.

---

# Operational Decision System

Each incident presents multiple response options.

Available actions include:

- Expedite Shipment
- Use Backup Supplier
- Reroute Trucks
- Increase Production
- Transfer Inventory
- Approve Air Freight
- Ignore
- Delay Decision

Every choice has different consequences across operational KPIs.

---

# Intelligent Business Logic

Correct decisions:

- Increase Score
- Improve Service Levels
- Protect Revenue
- Reduce Operational Risk

Poor decisions:

- Increase Operating Costs
- Reduce Customer Satisfaction
- Lower Inventory Health
- Decrease Revenue Protection

Some actions also trigger delayed consequences, requiring players to think beyond immediate results.

---

# Difficulty Progression

The simulation runs for **3 minutes**.

As time advances:

- Alert frequency increases
- Multiple incidents remain active simultaneously
- Critical alerts escalate rapidly
- Decision pressure becomes significantly higher

This mirrors the real environment of enterprise operations centers.

---

# Live Event Log

Every operational event is recorded in chronological order.

The log tracks:

- New Alerts
- Critical Incidents
- Resolved Events
- Missed Opportunities
- Score Updates

This creates a complete operational history throughout the simulation.

# Priority-Based Alert System

Alerts are categorized using enterprise-standard priority levels.

### Critical

- Immediate business impact
- Pulsing red indicators
- Highest urgency

### High

- Significant operational disruption
- Orange indicators

### Medium

- Moderate operational impact
- Blue indicators

Visual styling allows users to quickly identify the most urgent issues.
