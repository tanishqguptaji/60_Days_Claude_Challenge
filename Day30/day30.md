# Day 30 – Supply Chain Builder

## ABTalks 60 Days Claude Challenge

## Project Overview

Today I built **Supply Chain Builder**, an interactive business simulation that teaches the fundamentals of supply chain design through guided decision-making.

Instead of optimizing an existing enterprise, the simulator starts from scratch and allows users to build an entire supply chain while learning the trade-offs behind every business decision. Throughout the simulation, users receive beginner-friendly explanations before making choices and can observe how each decision impacts key operational metrics in real time.

The project combines supply chain education with interactive web development to create a realistic learning experience for aspiring operations managers.

---

# Objective

Develop a complete single-file React application that helps beginners understand how supply chains are designed and optimized through practical decision making.

---

# Features Implemented

## Interactive Welcome Experience

The simulator begins with a simple introduction explaining:

- What a supply chain is
- Why every business depends on one
- How trade-offs affect operations
- What users will learn during the simulation

The onboarding experience prepares users before they begin making decisions.

# Random Company Generator

Each playthrough creates a unique fictional company including:

- Company Name
- Industry
- Product Category
- Countries Served
- Demand Pattern

Examples include:

- Bicycle Manufacturer
- Electronics Company
- Food & Beverage Brand
- Furniture Manufacturer
- Consumer Goods Company

Because every simulation generates different business conditions, no two experiences are identical.

---

# Supply Chain Design Journey

Users progressively build the company's supply chain by making five major business decisions.

## 1. Supplier Strategy

Choose between:

- Single Supplier
- Multiple Suppliers

Users learn the balance between:

- Lower costs
- Simpler management
- Risk diversification
- Supply resilience

---

## 2. Factory Location

Select where production should occur.

Options include:

- Domestic
- Nearshore
- Offshore

Trade-offs include:

- Labor Cost
- Transportation Time
- Manufacturing Cost
- Sustainability
- Political Risk

---

## 3. Warehouse Strategy

Choose the inventory distribution model.

Available strategies:

- Centralized Warehouse
- Regional Warehouses
- Just-In-Time

The simulator explains:

- Inventory Costs
- Delivery Speed
- Customer Reach
- Operational Complexity

---

## 4. Transportation Method

Users select how products move across the supply chain.

Options:

- Road
- Rail
- Sea
- Air

Each option affects:

- Delivery Speed
- Cost
- Carbon Footprint
- Customer Satisfaction
- Supply Chain Risk

---

## 5. Inventory Strategy

Choose between:

- Low Inventory
- Balanced Inventory
- High Inventory

Users learn the relationship between:

- Working Capital
- Stock Availability
- Supply Risk
- Warehousing Costs

---

# Live Business Metrics

After every decision, the simulator updates key business KPIs in real time.

Tracked Metrics:

- Cost
- Delivery Speed
- Risk
- Customer Satisfaction
- Sustainability

Animated progress bars make it easy to visualize the business impact of each decision.

---

# Final Supply Chain Dashboard

Once all decisions are completed, the simulator generates a complete business report including:

- Overall Supply Chain Score
- Visual Score Meter
- Live KPI Dashboard
- Supply Chain Summary

The report helps users understand how their design choices affected overall performance.

---

# Performance Analysis

The simulator automatically identifies:

## Strengths

Examples:

- Low Operational Risk
- Strong Sustainability
- Fast Delivery
- Efficient Inventory

---

## Weaknesses

Examples:

- High Costs
- Customer Satisfaction Issues
- Slow Delivery
- Poor Sustainability

---

## Biggest Risk

The simulator highlights the weakest area within the supply chain and explains why it matters.

## Practical Recommendations

Three personalized improvement suggestions are generated based on the user's choices to encourage continuous optimization.
