# Day 37 – Task Compass

## ABTalks 60 Days Claude Challenge

## Project Overview

Today I built **Task Compass**, an interactive management simulation designed to teach how work flows through real organizations.

Instead of testing job titles or technical knowledge, the application focuses on understanding how ownership, delegation, workflow, and collaboration operate inside a modern workplace.

Players experience realistic workplace scenarios by assigning ownership, routing work through teams, and coordinating collaboration across departments. The goal is to develop organizational thinking through hands-on interaction rather than memorizing theory.

---

# Objective

Build a responsive, single-file offline web application that teaches organizational workflows through realistic workplace simulations.

The application demonstrates:

- Task ownership
- Delegation
- Workflow routing
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Organizational decision making

---

# Workplace Selected

**Tech Company**

The simulation recreates how engineering, product, QA, customer support, design, analytics, and management collaborate to solve real-world problems.

---

# Stage 1 – Who Owns This?

Users are presented with realistic workplace tickets and must identify the **primary owner** responsible for solving the issue.

Example scenarios include:

- Payments failing on iPhones
- Slow API response times
- Confusing signup flow
- Login failures
- Performance issues

After each decision, the application explains:

- Primary owner
- Why that role owns the problem
- Supporting roles that may assist

The focus is on understanding responsibility rather than simply marking answers as right or wrong.

---

# Stage 2 – Task Routing

Instead of choosing a single owner, users build the complete workflow showing how work moves through an organization.

Examples include:

Customer Support → Backend Developer → Engineering Manager → Customer Support

Different scenarios require different routing paths depending on the nature of the issue.

After submission the application:

- Animates workflow progression
- Explains why that sequence is commonly followed
- Demonstrates real organizational communication

This stage teaches that solving problems often requires structured handoffs rather than isolated work.

---

# Stage 3 – Collaboration Challenge

Users tackle larger organizational situations requiring multiple teams to work together.

Example scenarios include:

- Customer satisfaction suddenly drops
- Sales increase dramatically
- Negative product reviews appear online
- Large-scale product issues

Instead of assigning one owner, users assemble a cross-functional response team.

For every scenario the application explains:

- Primary owner
- Supporting teams
- Communication flow
- Why collaboration is essential

The simulation emphasizes that complex workplace challenges are solved through teamwork rather than individual effort.

---

# Organizational Thinking Dashboard

At the end of the experience, users receive an understanding dashboard instead of a traditional score.

Categories include:

- Ownership
- Delegation
- Collaboration
- Workflow Thinking

The dashboard provides a visual representation of organizational decision-making skills using animated progress bars.

---

# Reflection Summary

Rather than assigning grades, the application generates personalized insights including:

- What you understood well
- Where you tended to over-assign responsibility
- Where collaboration could improve
- One practical insight about how organizations actually work

The reflection encourages continuous learning instead of evaluation.
