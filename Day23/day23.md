# Day 23 – Startup Product Management with Claude

## Objective

Today's challenge focused on product management and customer discovery by transforming a validated startup idea into a practical Customer & MVP Blueprint.

Instead of building every possible feature, the goal was to identify:

- Ideal customers
- Customer pain points
- Buying behavior
- MVP scope
- Feature prioritization
- Pricing strategy
- Execution roadmap

The report follows a startup-first approach where customer validation comes before product development.

# Top Customer Pain Points

1. High prices for regular purchases
2. Fake or adulterated products
3. No trusted affordable brand
4. Stale products on shelves
5. Large pack sizes requiring higher spending
6. Lack of sourcing transparency
7. Poor packaging quality
8. Expensive gifting options
9. Difficult price comparison
10. No subscription or auto-replenishment

These insights reinforce that trust and affordability are the biggest opportunities.

Common concerns include:

- What if I don't like it?

Suggested solutions:

# Buying Triggers

Customers are most likely to purchase when offered:

- Trial pack (100g ≤ ₹49)
- Festival discounts (20–30%)
- Lab-tested quality badge
- Transparent sourcing story
- Referral cashback
- Subscription convenience

# MVP Recommendation

## Build First

- 100g & 250g Almond/Cashew SKUs
- Resealable pouch
- QR code for sourcing story
- WhatsApp Business ordering
- Amazon/Flipkart listing
- Manual fulfilment

---

## Do NOT Build Yet

- Mobile app
- Loyalty platform
- Full e-commerce website
- More than 4 SKUs
- Corporate gifting
- Subscription model

The report emphasizes validating demand before investing in technology.

# Key Learnings

This challenge highlighted that successful startups begin with customer understanding rather than feature development.

Major learnings include:

- Define ICP before building products
- Prioritize validation over perfection
- Launch with a focused MVP
- Measure repeat purchases instead of vanity metrics
- Use customer feedback to guide future iterations
