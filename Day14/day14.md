# Day 14 – AI Job Red Flag Detector

## What I Learned

Today I explored how AI can help evaluate job opportunities before investing time in applications and interviews.

Using Claude's Job Red Flag Detector, I analyzed an AI Engineer (LLM & Voice AI) role and generated a structured risk assessment.

The exercise helped me understand that evaluating employers is just as important as employers evaluating candidates.

---

# Top Red Flags

## Severity 7/10

### No Salary Information Provided

The job description does not disclose compensation details.

Potential Risk:

* Pay opacity
* Difficulty evaluating market competitiveness

---

## Severity 7/10

### Broad Scope of Responsibilities

The role combines:

* LLM Development
* Voice AI
* RAG Systems
* Telephony
* Deployment
* WebSockets

for a relatively junior-to-mid-level experience requirement.

Potential Risk:

* Scope creep
* Unrealistic expectations

---

## Severity 5/10

### Production Over Research Focus

The JD emphasizes production systems and shipping features quickly.

Potential Risk:

* High delivery pressure
* Limited research and experimentation opportunities

---

## Severity 5/10

### Missing Team & Growth Information

No information was provided regarding:

* Team size
* Reporting structure
* Engineering culture
* Career progression

Potential Risk:

* Unclear growth path
* Unknown work environment

# Smart Interview Questions

## Compensation & Scope

"The JD covers LLMs, Voice AI, RAG, telephony, and real-time systems. What does a typical sprint look like, and can you share the salary band for this role?"

---

## Team Ownership

"How large is the AI engineering team, and who would I collaborate with on the Voice AI stack?"

---

## Production Pressure

"The role emphasizes production systems. What does the on-call process and incident management workflow look like?"

---

## Career Growth

"What does career progression look like for AI Engineers within the organization?"

---

## Research Opportunities

"How much time is allocated for experimentation, learning, and AI research compared to production delivery?"

---

# Biggest Observation

The most surprising insight was that a role can have:

* Excellent technology
* Strong company backing
* High-impact work

while simultaneously having risks related to:

* Hiring transparency
* Scope expectations
* Team structure

Evaluating both sides provides a more balanced career decision.

---

# Key Learnings

* Job applications are a two-way evaluation process.
* Salary transparency matters.
* Scope creep can be identified from JDs.
* Team structure should always be clarified.
* AI can help evaluate opportunities objectively.

---


# Final Insight

The best career decisions are not made by applying everywhere.

They are made by identifying opportunities that align with:

* Skills
* Career goals
* Growth expectations
* Work-life balance
* Compensation transparency

AI can significantly improve that evaluation process.
